## What Is Bitcoin Dominance?

Bitcoin Dominance (BTC.D) measures Bitcoin's market capitalization as a percentage of the total cryptocurrency market cap. It shows how much of the crypto market value is held in Bitcoin versus altcoins.

**Key Concept:** BTC dominance reflects risk appetite and capital flows within the crypto ecosystem.

## Calculating Bitcoin Dominance

Example:
Bitcoin market cap: $2 trillion
Total crypto market cap: $3.5 trillion

## What Dominance Tells Us

### High Dominance (>60%)

**Characteristics:**
- Capital concentrated in Bitcoin
- Risk-off sentiment in crypto
- Flight to quality
- Early bull or late bear market

**Trading Implications:**
- Focus on Bitcoin
- Altcoin opportunities limited
- Safer market conditions
- Potential alt season ahead

### Low Dominance (<45%)

**Characteristics:**
- Capital spread across altcoins
- Risk-on sentiment
- Speculation elevated
- Often late-cycle euphoria

**Trading Implications:**
- Altcoins may outperform
- Higher risk environment
- Potential market top forming
- Consider taking profits

### Moderate Dominance (45-55%)

**Characteristics:**
- Balanced market
- No strong directional signal
- Rotation possible
- Transition period

## Trading with Dominance

### Strategy 1: Dominance Reversal Trade

**Long Bitcoin Signal:**
- BTC.D hits multi-month low
- Shows reversal pattern (double bottom, bullish divergence)
- Altcoins showing weakness
- Action: Rotate from alts to BTC

**Long Altcoins Signal:**
- BTC.D hits multi-month high
- Shows reversal pattern
- Bitcoin consolidating after rally
- Action: Consider alt exposure

**Bearish Alt Divergence:**
- Alt prices rising
- BTC.D rising
- Signal: Alts underperforming on strength = relative weakness

## Dominance Considerations

### The "Stablecoin Problem"

Stablecoins (USDT, USDC) affect dominance calculation:

```
If stablecoins are 10% of market cap:
- They dilute BTC dominance
- But they're not competing for capital
- Some analysts exclude stablecoins
- "Real" BTC dominance may be higher
```

### New Tokens Inflation

Every new token launch dilutes BTC dominance:
- Thousands of new tokens yearly
- Most have minimal value
- Creates downward bias in dominance
- Historical comparisons need context

### Market Cap vs. Liquidity

- Many altcoins have inflated caps

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Trading Dominance in Isolation

**Mistake:** "BTC.D is low, time to buy Bitcoin"
**Reality:** Low dominance can stay low. Need trend change confirmation.

### 2. Ignoring Absolute Prices

**Mistake:** "BTC.D rising, Bitcoin is doing well"
**Reality:** Dominance can rise while both BTC and alts fall (BTC just falls less).

### 3. Over-Extrapolating History

**Mistake:** "Dominance always returns to 60%+"
**Reality:** Market structure evolves. Historical levels may not repeat.

### 4. Precision Trading

**Mistake:** Trading every 1% dominance move
**Reality:** Use dominance for broader bias, not scalping.

## Summary

Bitcoin dominance provides macro context for crypto markets:

- **High dominance** = Safety preference, early cycle or bear market
- **Low dominance** = Risk appetite, late cycle or alt season
- **Rising dominance** = Favor Bitcoin over alts
- **Falling dominance** = Consider alt exposure
- **Extreme levels** = Watch for reversals

Use dominance as a filter for allocation decisions, not as a standalone trading signal.
