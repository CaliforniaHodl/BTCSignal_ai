## Why Psychology Matters

Trading is 80% psychology and 20% strategy. You can have the best system in the world, but if you can't execute it consistently, you'll fail.

**The Truth:** Most traders lose not because of bad analysis, but because of emotional decisions.

## The Two Enemies: Fear and Greed

### Fear

Fear manifests as:

| Fear Type | Behavior | Result |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Fear of losing | Taking profits too early | Small wins, big losses |
| Fear of missing out (FOMO) | Chasing pumps | Buying tops |
| Fear of being wrong | Not taking stop losses | Blowing accounts |
| Fear of pulling trigger | Missing good setups | Frustration |

### Greed

Greed manifests as:

| Greed Type | Behavior | Result |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Wanting more | Not taking profits | Winners become losers |
| Overconfidence | Oversizing positions | Catastrophic losses |
| Revenge trading | Doubling down after losses | Account destruction |
| Impatience | Overtrading | Death by 1000 cuts |

## Common Cognitive Biases

### 1. Confirmation Bias

**What it is:** Seeking information that confirms your existing belief.

**In trading:** Only reading bullish analysis when you're long.

**Solution:** Actively seek opposing viewpoints. Ask "why might I be wrong?"

### 2. Recency Bias

**What it is:** Overweighting recent events.

**In trading:** After 5 winning trades, feeling invincible. After 5 losses, feeling like you'll never win.

**Solution:** Look at your complete trading history, not just recent trades.

### 3. Anchoring Bias

**What it is:** Fixating on a specific price.

**In trading:** "BTC should go back to $69K because that's where it was."

**Solution:** Trade what IS, not what WAS. The market doesn't care about old prices.

### 4. Loss Aversion

**What it is:** Losses hurt more than gains feel good (roughly 2.5x more).

**In trading:** Holding losers too long, cutting winners too short.

**Solution:** Think in terms of R (risk units), not dollars. A 1R loss is just a cost of business.

### 5. Sunk Cost Fallacy

**What it is:** Continuing a losing position because you've already invested.

**In trading:** "I can't sell now, I'm already down 30%."

**Solution:** Ask "Would I enter this trade today?" If no, exit.

### 6. Hindsight Bias

**What it is:** Believing you "knew it all along" after the fact.

**In trading:** "I knew BTC would dump from $69K."

**Solution:** Keep a trading journal with your REAL thoughts before trades.

## The Psychology of Winning and Losing

### After a Win

**Dangerous thoughts:**
- "I'm a genius"
- "I should size up"
- "This is easy"

**Reality:** One win means nothing statistically. Stay humble.

**Action:** Follow your plan. Same size. Same rules.

### After a Loss

**Dangerous thoughts:**
- "I need to make it back"
- "The market is rigged"
- "I'll size up to recover faster"

**Reality:** Losses are part of the game. Even 40% win rate can be profitable.

**Action:** Take a break. Review the trade. Was it a good loss (followed plan) or bad loss (broke rules)?

### Good Losses vs Bad Losses

| Good Loss | Bad Loss |
|-----------|----------|
| Followed your plan | Broke your rules |
| Proper position size | Oversized |
| Stop loss hit | No stop / moved stop |
| Would take same trade again | Emotional entry |

Good losses are acceptable. Bad losses must be eliminated.

## Building a Trading Routine

### Pre-Market Routine

1. **Check your state:** Rested? Stressed? Emotional?
2. **Review the market:** What happened overnight?
3. **Identify key levels:** Where are the important S/R zones?
4. **Plan scenarios:** "If X happens, I'll do Y"
5. **Set alerts:** Don't stare at charts

### During Trading

1. **Follow your plan:** No improvisation
2. **Size appropriately:** Never bet too big
3. **Respect stops:** No exceptions
4. **Take breaks:** Every 2-4 hours minimum

### Post-Market Routine

1. **Log all trades:** Entry, exit, reason, emotion
2. **Review execution:** Did you follow the plan?
3. **Identify errors:** What could improve?
4. **Prepare for tomorrow:** Set up charts/alerts

## The Trading Journal

A trading journal is the most powerful tool for improvement.

### What to Record

| Field | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------------|
| Date/Time | Identify time-based patterns |
| Asset & Timeframe | Track what works best |
| Direction | Long/short tendencies |
| Entry Price | Execution quality |
| Stop Loss | Risk management |
| Take Profit | Profit taking habits |
| Position Size | Sizing discipline |
| R:R | Quality of setups |
| Outcome | Win/loss amount |
| Emotion Before | Identify emotional trading |
| Emotion During | Spot patterns |
| Emotion After | Track recovery |
| Notes | What did you learn? |

### Weekly Review Questions

1. Did I follow my rules?
2. What was my best trade? Why?
3. What was my worst trade? Why?
4. What patterns do I see in my losses?
5. What one thing can I improve next week?

## Managing Tilt

Tilt is the emotional state where you make irrational decisions.

### Signs You're on Tilt

- Trading more frequently
- Increasing position sizes
- Ignoring your rules
- Feeling angry or frustrated
- Thinking "I'll show the market"
- Checking P&L constantly

### Tilt Recovery Protocol

1. **Stop trading immediately**
2. **Close all positions** (optional but often wise)
3. **Step away from charts** (minimum 1 hour)
4. **Physical activity** (walk, exercise)
5. **Review what triggered tilt**
6. **Return only when calm**

## Position Sizing and Psychology

Your position size directly affects your psychology:

| Position Size | Psychological Effect |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Too small | Boredom, overtrading |
| Correct | Calm, rational decisions |
| Too large | Fear, unable to let winners run |
| Way too large | Panic, guaranteed poor decisions |

**Rule:** If you're constantly checking your P&L, your size is too big.

## Building Discipline

### The 10 Rules of Discipline

1. **Always use a stop loss**
2. **Never move stop loss further from entry**
3. **Risk maximum 1-2% per trade**
4. **Follow your trading plan exactly**
5. **No revenge trading**
6. **Take planned breaks**
7. **Log every trade**
8. **Review weekly**
9. **Stay humble after wins**
10. **Stay confident after losses**

### Breaking Rules = Tax

Treat rule breaks as a "tax" on your account:
- Broke a rule? Donate $50 to charity
- Creates a real cost to bad behavior
- Makes you think twice

## The Professional Mindset

### Amateur vs Professional

| Amateur | Professional |
|---------|--------------|
| Seeks excitement | Seeks consistency |
| Trades to make money | Trades to follow process |
| Emotional after trades | Detached from outcomes |
| Skips journaling | Journals religiously |
| Blames the market | Takes responsibility |
| Focuses on P&L | Focuses on execution |

### Think Like a Casino

Casinos don't care about individual bets because they have an edge:

- Individual trades don't matter
- The edge plays out over many trades
- Variance is expected
- Losses are a cost of business

You need enough trades for your edge to manifest. Stay in the game.

4. "Losses are part of the process"

6. "I am patient and disciplined"
