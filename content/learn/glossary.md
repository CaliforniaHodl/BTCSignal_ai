## A

**All-Time High (ATH)** - The highest price ever recorded for an asset. Bitcoin's ATH changes with each new cycle peak.

**Altcoin** - Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. Short for "alternative coin."

**Accumulation** - A market phase where smart money is buying while prices are low or stable.

## B

**Bear Market** - Extended period of declining prices, typically 20%+ from recent highs.

**Bitcoin (BTC)** - The first and largest cryptocurrency, created by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009.

**Block** - A collection of Bitcoin transactions that are confirmed together. New blocks are added approximately every 10 minutes.

**Block Reward** - Bitcoin paid to miners for successfully mining a block. Currently 3.125 BTC, halving every ~4 years.

**Bollinger Bands** - Technical indicator showing price volatility using standard deviations from a moving average.

**Bull Market** - Extended period of rising prices and positive market sentiment.

## C

**Candlestick** - A price chart element showing open, high, low, and close prices for a time period.

**Cold Storage** - Storing Bitcoin offline, typically on hardware wallets, for maximum security.

**Correction** - A decline of 10%+ from recent highs, often healthy in uptrends.

**Cross Margin** - Using entire account balance as collateral for leveraged positions.

## D

**DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)** - Investment strategy of buying fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price.

**Dip** - A temporary price decline within an overall uptrend.

**Divergence** - When price and an indicator (like RSI) move in opposite directions, often signaling reversals.

**Dominance** - Bitcoin's market cap as a percentage of total crypto market cap. Higher = BTC strength.

## E

**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)** - Moving average giving more weight to recent prices.

**Exchange** - Platform for buying, selling, and trading Bitcoin.

## F

**Fair Value Gap (FVG)** - Price imbalance on a chart where no trading occurred, often revisited later.

**Fear & Greed Index** - Sentiment indicator measuring market emotions from 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed).

**Fiat** - Government-issued currency like USD, EUR, or JPY.

**FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)** - Emotional buying driven by fear of missing price gains.

**FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)** - Negative information spread to create panic selling.

**Funding Rate** - Periodic payment between long and short traders on perpetual futures.

## H

**Halving** - Bitcoin event cutting block rewards in half, occurring every 210,000 blocks (~4 years).

**Hardware Wallet** - Physical device for secure Bitcoin storage (e.g., Ledger, Trezor).

**Hash Rate** - Total computational power securing the Bitcoin network.

**HODL** - "Hold On for Dear Life" - Long-term holding strategy regardless of price swings.

## I

**Isolated Margin** - Using specific collateral per position, limiting potential loss.

## L

**Leverage** - Trading with borrowed funds to amplify position size and potential gains/losses.

**Lightning Network** - Layer 2 solution for fast, cheap Bitcoin transactions.

**Liquidation** - Forced closure of a leveraged position when collateral is insufficient.

**Liquidity** - Ease of buying or selling without significantly affecting price.

**Long** - Betting on price increase. "Going long" means buying.

## M

**MACD** - Moving Average Convergence Divergence. Trend-following momentum indicator.

**Market Cap** - Total value of all circulating Bitcoin (price Ã— supply).

**Mempool** - Queue of unconfirmed Bitcoin transactions waiting to be added to blocks.

**Miner** - Entity that validates transactions and creates new blocks.

**MVRV** - Market Value to Realized Value ratio. On-chain valuation metric.

## N

**Node** - Computer running Bitcoin software, validating transactions and blocks.

**NUPL** - Net Unrealized Profit/Loss. On-chain metric showing aggregate market profitability.

## O

**On-Chain** - Analysis using data directly from the blockchain.

**Open Interest** - Total outstanding derivative contracts (futures/options).

**Order Block** - Institutional buying/selling zone, often acts as support/resistance.

## P

**Paper Hands** - Selling at the first sign of trouble. Opposite of diamond hands.

**Perpetual Futures (Perps)** - Futures contracts with no expiration date.

**Position Sizing** - Determining how much capital to allocate per trade.

**Private Key** - Secret code controlling Bitcoin ownership. Never share.

## R

**Realized Cap** - Total value of all Bitcoin at the price each coin last moved.

**Resistance** - Price level where selling pressure tends to prevent further rise.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)** - Momentum oscillator measuring overbought/oversold conditions.

## S

**Satoshi (sat)** - Smallest Bitcoin unit. 1 BTC = 100,000,000 sats.

**Satoshi Nakamoto** - Pseudonymous creator of Bitcoin.

**Short** - Betting on price decrease. "Going short" means selling borrowed assets.

**SMA (Simple Moving Average)** - Average price over a set number of periods.

**SOPR** - Spent Output Profit Ratio. On-chain metric showing profit/loss of moving coins.

**Stop Loss** - Order to automatically sell if price drops to limit losses.

**Support** - Price level where buying pressure tends to prevent further decline.

**Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)** - Price briefly breaks a level then reverses, trapping traders.

## T

**Take Profit** - Order to automatically sell when price reaches target.

**Technical Analysis (TA)** - Analyzing price charts and indicators to predict future movements.

**Trading Volume** - Amount of Bitcoin traded in a given period.

## U

**UTXO** - Unspent Transaction Output. The way Bitcoin tracks ownership.

## V

**Volatility** - Degree of price fluctuation. Bitcoin is notably volatile.

**Volume Profile** - Shows trading activity at different price levels.

## W

**Wallet** - Software or hardware for storing Bitcoin private keys.

**Whale** - Entity holding large amounts of Bitcoin (typically 1,000+ BTC).

**Wick** - The thin lines above/below candlestick bodies showing price extremes.
