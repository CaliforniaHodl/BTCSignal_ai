## What Is Dollar Cost Averaging?

Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals, regardless of price. This eliminates the stress of timing the market.

**Key Concept:** DCA turns Bitcoin's volatility from an enemy into an ally by automatically buying more when prices are low.

## How DCA Works

### The Math

Month 1: BTC at $50,000 â†’ Buy 0.01 BTC

## DCA vs Lump Sum

### When Lump Sum Wins

- You have perfect market timing (unlikely)
- Market is in clear uptrend
- Time horizon is very long

### When DCA Wins

- You're uncertain about direction
- Market is volatile
- You want to sleep at night
- You're investing regular income

### The Psychology Advantage

| Scenario | Lump Sum Feeling | DCA Feeling |
|----------|------------------|-------------|
| Price drops 30% | "I lost $30,000" | "Great, buying cheaper" |
| Price rises 30% | "I'm a genius" | "Wish I bought more" |
| Price goes sideways | "Was this right?" | "Accumulating steadily" |

**Reality:** Most investors struggle with lump sum timing. DCA removes emotion.

## Common DCA Mistakes

### 1. Stopping During Bear Markets

**Mistake:** "Price is crashing, I'll wait"
**Reality:** Bear markets are when DCA works best

### 2. Trying to Time Within DCA

**Mistake:** "I'll skip this week and buy next week when it's lower"
**Reality:** This defeats the purpose of DCA

### 3. DCAing Money You Need

**Mistake:** Investing emergency fund or bill money
**Reality:** Only DCA with truly long-term capital

### 4. Not Having an Exit Strategy

**Mistake:** "I'll just DCA forever"
**Reality:** Have a plan for eventual profit-taking

### 5. Checking Price Too Often

**Mistake:** Daily price anxiety
**Reality:** Set it, forget it, check monthly
